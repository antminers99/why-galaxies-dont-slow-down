  a₀ ≈ 1.2×10⁻¹⁰ m/s² ≈ 3702 (km/s)²/kpc

No galaxy property correlates with a₀:

Below a₀, the gravity deficit is always compensated — and the compensation

Not on surface brightness, not on mass, not on size, not on morphology.
A single function of one variable describes every galaxy we tested.

  Predicted:  cH₀/2π = 1.08×10⁻¹⁰ m/s²

This is either a deep physical connection or a remarkable numerical

PART II: THE ACCELERATION FLOOR
================================================================================

Below a₀, gravity is always compensated — identically, in every galaxy,
regardless of its mass, size, or type. The compensation follows a single
universal rule and the threshold happens to equal the speed of light
times the Hubble constant, divided by 2π.

Three remarkable properties of the floor:

1. IT IS UNIVERSAL — The same floor applies to every galaxy. Tiny dwarfs
   with V_max ~ 40 km/s and giant spirals with V_max ~ 250 km/s show
   the same transition scale, despite vastly different formation histories.

2. IT IS SIMPLE — The compensation is a function of one variable (g_bar)
   only. We tried adding surface brightness, mass, and size as second
   variables — they all make the prediction WORSE, not better.

3. IT IS COSMOLOGICAL — The floor value (a₀) equals cH₀/2π within ~10%.
   This connects a purely local measurement (galaxy rotation curves) to
   the largest scale in physics (the expansion of the universe).

In plain language: Below a certain acceleration threshold, something always
compensates the missing gravity — identically, in every galaxy, regardless
of mass, size, or type. The compensation follows a single universal rule.
And the threshold happens to match the expansion rate of the universe.

PART III: THE EQUATION
================================================================================

STARTING POINT
--------------
Two measurable accelerations at every point in every galaxy:
  g_bar = GM_bar(r) / r²    (baryonic prediction)
  g_obs = V²(r) / r          (observed from rotation curve)

CORE EQUATION (Acceleration Floor Form)
----------------------------------------
  g_obs² = g_bar² + a₀² × ν(g_bar/a₀)

where ν(x) = x/(1+x) is a smooth interpolation function.

EXPANDED FORM
-------------
  g_obs = √(g_bar² + a₀² × g_bar / (g_bar + a₀))

VELOCITY FORM (for fitting rotation curves)
--------------------------------------------
  V(r) = √(g_obs × r)
       = √(r × √((GM/r²)² + a₀² × (GM/r²) / (GM/r² + a₀)))

COMPLETE EQUATION (No Extra Tuning Parameters)
------------------------------------------------
  g_obs = √(g_bar² + (cH₀/2π)² × g_bar / (g_bar + cH₀/2π))

Given fixed baryonic mass-to-light assumptions (Υ_disk = 0.5, Υ_bulge = 0.7)
and standard cosmological inputs (c, H₀), the equation adds no extra
galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters. Every quantity is either measured
locally (g_bar, which depends on adopted Υ and distance) or fixed by
cosmology (c, H₀).

NOTE: The equation is not "parameter-free" in the absolute sense. It
inherits systematic dependence on Υ assumptions, distance calibrations,
and gas mass modeling. What it avoids is any per-galaxy free parameter
analogous to a halo concentration or core radius.

PART IV: LIMIT VERIFICATION
================================================================================

HIGH ACCELERATION (Newtonian Limit)
------------------------------------
When g_bar >> a₀:
  ν → 1, so g_obs² ≈ g_bar² + a₀² ≈ g_bar²
  Therefore: g_obs = g_bar  ✓ (Newton recovered)

LOW ACCELERATION (Deep MOND Limit)
------------------------------------
When g_bar << a₀:
  ν → g_bar/a₀, so g_obs² ≈ g_bar × a₀
  Therefore: g_obs = √(g_bar × a₀)  ✓ (RAR recovered)

PART V: TULLY-FISHER DERIVATION
================================================================================

In the deep low-acceleration regime (outer galaxy):

Step 1: g_obs ≈ √(g_bar × a₀)
Step 2: V²/r = √(GM/r² × a₀)
Step 3: V⁴ = G × M_bar × a₀

This is the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation — DERIVED, not assumed.
The flat rotation velocity scales as M^(1/4), exactly as observed.

With a₀ = 3702 (km/s)²/kpc:
  V_flat = (G × M × a₀)^(1/4)

This is a strong consistency check: the equation naturally reproduces
one of the most important observational relations in galaxy physics.

PART XIII: THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK (k → Density Profile)
================================================================================

Deriving the physical meaning of k from first principles.
Why does the universe impose k ≈ V²/R?

DERIVATION: FROM k TO DARK MATTER DENSITY

Step 1: Start with empirical model
  v² = GM/r + kr
  First term = standard Newtonian gravity
  Second term (kr) grows linearly with radius

Step 2: Extract dark matter acceleration
  a_dark = v²_dark/r = k (constant!)
  A constant acceleration field independent of radius

Step 3: Derive enclosed dark mass
  M_dark(r) = k·r² / G
  Dark mass grows as r²

Step 4: Derive density profile ρ(r)
  From dM/dr = 2kr/G and ρ = (dM/dr) / (4πr²):
  ρ(r) = k / (2πGr)
  Dark matter density drops as 1/r — the NFW CUSP profile!

Step 5: Density amplitude A
  A = k / (2πG), so ρ(r) = A/r
  k = 2πGA → k is directly proportional to central density amplitude

Step 6: Empirical scaling k ≈ V²/R
  At R_max, the dark contribution v²_dark = kR sets the velocity scale.
  k = V²_max/R_max with R² ≈ 0.896 across 175 galaxies

Step 7: Full picture
  ρ(r) ≈ V²_max / (2πG · R_max · r)
  Dark matter density at any radius is determined by two observables.

VERIFICATION
  log₁₀(k) vs log₁₀(V²/R): power law slope ≈ 1.0
  R² ≈ 0.896 — 89.6% variance explained

DENSITY PROFILE IMPLICATIONS
  The derived ρ(R) ≈ (2/3) × ρ_virial — consistent with
  gravitational equilibrium.

OPEN QUESTIONS
  1. Dark matter or modified gravity? The 1/r profile is consistent
     with both CDM halos and certain modified gravity theories.
  2. Does the 1/r profile extend to very small radii?
     The cusp-core problem suggests it may flatten.
  3. Validation on independent data: THINGS, LITTLE THINGS, EDGES
     surveys should confirm k = V²/R.
  4. Cosmological constraints: Does ρ ∝ 1/r integrate consistently
     with CMB, BAO, and large-scale structure?
  5. Theoretical derivation: Can 1/r be derived from first principles?

Based on analysis of 175 SPARC galaxies (+ 22 LITTLE THINGS dwarfs),
4,123 rotation-curve measurements, using an empirical relation with no
extra galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters beyond fixed baryonic inputs:

The data strongly supports the existence of a characteristic acceleration
scale a₀ in galaxy dynamics that shows no significant dependence on any
tested galaxy property. This scale matches cH₀/2π within ~10%.
These are robust empirical regularities.

The apparent dark matter fraction is strongly anti-correlated with baryonic
surface density (nominal combined significance: 5.1σ over ΛCDM expectations,
frequentist bootstrap). This coupling extends beyond the mass fraction to

Σ_bar. This result is confirmed independently on LITTLE THINGS dwarfs and
is consistent with all seven systematic robustness checks.

A first-pass cluster stress test shows that the same floor scale (a₀ from

correct dynamical ballpark, reducing the Newtonian deficit by ~43%. However,
~52.5% residual dark matter fraction remains. The floor does not provide a
distinct or complete cluster-scale solution and remains nearly degenerate

normalization should shift upward by E(z)^(1/4) ≈ 14% at fixed baryonic

Whether this implies modified gravity, a new dark matter phase, or emergent
cosmological coupling remains an open question that requires:
  1. High-redshift rotation curves (JWST/ALMA)
  2. Galaxy cluster deep analysis
  3. Wide binary observations (Gaia DR4)
  4. Theoretical mechanism

The equation:
  g_obs = √(g_bar² + (cH₀/2π)² × g_bar/(g_bar + cH₀/2π))

captures the observation with no extra galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters

The hardest question — is this a new law of nature, or an emergent
