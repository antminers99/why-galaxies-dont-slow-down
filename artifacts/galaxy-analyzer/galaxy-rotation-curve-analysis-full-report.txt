No galaxy property correlates with a₀:

Below a₀, the gravity deficit is always compensated — and the compensation

Not on surface brightness, not on mass, not on size, not on morphology.
A single function of one variable describes every galaxy we tested.

This is either a deep physical connection or a remarkable numerical

PART II: THE ACCELERATION FLOOR
================================================================================

Below a₀, gravity is always compensated — identically, in every galaxy,
regardless of its mass, size, or type. The compensation follows a single
universal rule and the threshold happens to equal the speed of light
times the Hubble constant, divided by 2π.

Three remarkable properties of the floor:

1. IT IS UNIVERSAL — The same floor applies to every galaxy. Tiny dwarfs
   with V_max ~ 40 km/s and giant spirals with V_max ~ 250 km/s show
   the same transition scale, despite vastly different formation histories.

2. IT IS SIMPLE — The compensation is a function of one variable (g_bar)
   only. We tried adding surface brightness, mass, and size as second
   variables — they all make the prediction WORSE, not better.

3. IT IS COSMOLOGICAL — The floor value (a₀) equals cH₀/2π within ~10%.
   This connects a purely local measurement (galaxy rotation curves) to
   the largest scale in physics (the expansion of the universe).

In plain language: Below a certain acceleration threshold, something always
compensates the missing gravity — identically, in every galaxy, regardless
of mass, size, or type. The compensation follows a single universal rule.
And the threshold happens to match the expansion rate of the universe.

PART III: THE EQUATION
================================================================================

STARTING POINT
--------------
Two measurable accelerations at every point in every galaxy:
  g_bar = GM_bar(r) / r²    (baryonic prediction)
  g_obs = V²(r) / r          (observed from rotation curve)

CORE EQUATION (Acceleration Floor Form)
----------------------------------------
  g_obs² = g_bar² + a₀² × ν(g_bar/a₀)

where ν(x) = x/(1+x) is a smooth interpolation function.

EXPANDED FORM
-------------
  g_obs = √(g_bar² + a₀² × g_bar / (g_bar + a₀))

VELOCITY FORM (for fitting rotation curves)
--------------------------------------------
  V(r) = √(g_obs × r)
       = √(r × √((GM/r²)² + a₀² × (GM/r²) / (GM/r² + a₀)))

COMPLETE EQUATION (No Extra Tuning Parameters)
------------------------------------------------
  g_obs = √(g_bar² + (cH₀/2π)² × g_bar / (g_bar + cH₀/2π))

Given fixed baryonic mass-to-light assumptions (Υ_disk = 0.5, Υ_bulge = 0.7)
and standard cosmological inputs (c, H₀), the equation adds no extra
galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters. Every quantity is either measured
locally (g_bar, which depends on adopted Υ and distance) or fixed by
cosmology (c, H₀).

NOTE: The equation is not "parameter-free" in the absolute sense. It
inherits systematic dependence on Υ assumptions, distance calibrations,
and gas mass modeling. What it avoids is any per-galaxy free parameter
analogous to a halo concentration or core radius.

PART IV: LIMIT VERIFICATION
================================================================================

HIGH ACCELERATION (Newtonian Limit)
------------------------------------
When g_bar >> a₀:
  ν → 1, so g_obs² ≈ g_bar² + a₀² ≈ g_bar²
  Therefore: g_obs = g_bar  ✓ (Newton recovered)

LOW ACCELERATION (Deep MOND Limit)
------------------------------------
When g_bar << a₀:
  ν → g_bar/a₀, so g_obs² ≈ g_bar × a₀
  Therefore: g_obs = √(g_bar × a₀)  ✓ (RAR recovered)

PART V: TULLY-FISHER DERIVATION
================================================================================

In the deep low-acceleration regime (outer galaxy):

Step 1: g_obs ≈ √(g_bar × a₀)
Step 2: V²/r = √(GM/r² × a₀)
Step 3: V⁴ = G × M_bar × a₀

This is the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation — DERIVED, not assumed.
The flat rotation velocity scales as M^(1/4), exactly as observed.

With a₀ = 3702 (km/s)²/kpc:
  V_flat = (G × M × a₀)^(1/4)

This is a strong consistency check: the equation naturally reproduces
one of the most important observational relations in galaxy physics.

PART XIII: THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK (k → Density Profile)
================================================================================

Deriving the physical meaning of k from first principles.
Why does the universe impose k ≈ V²/R?

DERIVATION: FROM k TO DARK MATTER DENSITY

Step 1: Start with empirical model
  v² = GM/r + kr
  First term = standard Newtonian gravity
  Second term (kr) grows linearly with radius

Step 2: Extract dark matter acceleration
  a_dark = v²_dark/r = k (constant!)
  A constant acceleration field independent of radius

Step 3: Derive enclosed dark mass
  M_dark(r) = k·r² / G
  Dark mass grows as r²

Step 4: Derive density profile ρ(r)
  From dM/dr = 2kr/G and ρ = (dM/dr) / (4πr²):
  ρ(r) = k / (2πGr)
  Dark matter density drops as 1/r — the NFW CUSP profile!

Step 5: Density amplitude A
  A = k / (2πG), so ρ(r) = A/r
  k = 2πGA → k is directly proportional to central density amplitude

Step 6: Empirical scaling k ≈ V²/R
  At R_max, the dark contribution v²_dark = kR sets the velocity scale.
  k = V²_max/R_max with R² ≈ 0.896 across 175 galaxies

Step 7: Full picture
  ρ(r) ≈ V²_max / (2πG · R_max · r)
  Dark matter density at any radius is determined by two observables.

VERIFICATION
  log₁₀(k) vs log₁₀(V²/R): power law slope ≈ 1.0
  R² ≈ 0.896 — 89.6% variance explained

DENSITY PROFILE IMPLICATIONS
  The derived ρ(R) ≈ (2/3) × ρ_virial — consistent with
  gravitational equilibrium.

OPEN QUESTIONS
  1. Dark matter or modified gravity? The 1/r profile is consistent
     with both CDM halos and certain modified gravity theories.
  2. Does the 1/r profile extend to very small radii?
     The cusp-core problem suggests it may flatten.
  3. Validation on independent data: THINGS, LITTLE THINGS, EDGES
     surveys should confirm k = V²/R.
  4. Cosmological constraints: Does ρ ∝ 1/r integrate consistently
     with CMB, BAO, and large-scale structure?
  5. Theoretical derivation: Can 1/r be derived from first principles?

================================================================================

Based on analysis of 175 SPARC galaxies (+ 22 LITTLE THINGS dwarfs),
4,123 rotation-curve measurements, using an empirical relation with no
extra galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters beyond fixed baryonic inputs:

The data strongly supports the existence of a characteristic acceleration
scale a₀ in galaxy dynamics that shows no significant dependence on any
tested galaxy property. This scale matches cH₀/2π within ~10%.
These are robust empirical regularities.

The apparent dark matter fraction is strongly anti-correlated with baryonic
surface density (nominal combined significance: 5.1σ over ΛCDM expectations,
frequentist bootstrap). This coupling extends beyond the mass fraction to
the halo structure itself — V_DM, r_DMdom, and the inner slope all track
Σ_bar. This result is confirmed independently on LITTLE THINGS dwarfs and
is consistent with all seven systematic robustness checks.

A first-pass cluster stress test shows that the same floor scale (a₀ from

correct dynamical ballpark, reducing the Newtonian deficit by ~43%. However,
~52.5% residual dark matter fraction remains. The floor does not provide a
distinct or complete cluster-scale solution and remains nearly degenerate
with MOND in this regime. Additional physics (hot gas, real DM, or
cluster-specific effects) is required.

The model makes a unique, falsifiable prediction: in the Cosmic Floor
hypothesis, the effective acceleration scale inferred from galaxy dynamics
should scale with H(z). For a flat ΛCDM background with Ω_m = 0.315,
this implies a₀(z=1) ≈ 1.70 × a₀(z=0), i.e. ~70% larger, and the BTFR
normalization should shift upward by E(z)^(1/4) ≈ 14% at fixed baryonic
mass. JWST and ALMA can test this within the next few years.

Whether this implies modified gravity, a new dark matter phase, or emergent
cosmological coupling remains an open question that requires:
  1. High-redshift rotation curves (JWST/ALMA)
  2. Galaxy cluster deep analysis
  3. Wide binary observations (Gaia DR4)
  4. Theoretical mechanism

The equation:
  g_obs = √(g_bar² + (cH₀/2π)² × g_bar/(g_bar + cH₀/2π))

captures the observation with no extra galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters
(given standard baryonic inputs). But having the right equation is not the
same as understanding the physics.

We do not know WHY a₀ exists.
The data robustly indicates it is real, universal within our sample, and
cosmologically suggestive. It equals cH₀/2π within ~10%. No tested galaxy
property improves or modulates it.

The hardest question — is this a new law of nature, or an emergent
consequence of self-regulation? — remains open. The current data
cannot distinguish between the two.

Any viable theory of galaxy dynamics must explain why a characteristic
scale this close to cH₀/2π appears in the data:

  a₀ = cH₀ / 2π

PART XXVI-C: THE THREE PATHS
================================================================================

  Three derivation paths, evaluated against the 6 conditions (C1–C6).

  ──── PATH 1: de Sitter / Horizon ────

  Core argument:
    In de Sitter space, there is a cosmological horizon at R = c/H.
    This horizon has a surface gravity κ = c√(Λ/3) = H_∞·c.
    Any particle in a de Sitter universe cannot be accelerated
    below κ without falling below the horizon's own gravitational
    scale. Therefore a_min = κ = c√(Λ/3).

  What it explains:
    - Global scale: a₀ comes from cosmology, not galaxy properties
    - Universality: same Λ everywhere → same a₀ everywhere
    - Newton limit: κ only matters when g_bar → 0

  What it fails:
    - Numerical match: c√(Λ/3) = 7.07×10⁻¹¹, needs factor ~1.7 to reach a₀
    - 2π: does not naturally produce the 2π factor
    - Mechanism: surface gravity of a horizon is not the same as
      modifying dynamics inside a galaxy. How does the horizon "reach in"?
    - Clusters: no prediction for the ~52.5% residual
    - Redshift: predicts constant a₀ (Λ-model), not H-evolving

  Open questions specific to this path:
    - Is the factor 1.7 fixable, or is it a sign of a wrong framework?
    - Can horizon thermodynamics provide the mechanism?

  ──── PATH 2: Unruh / Thermodynamic ────

  Core argument:
    A uniformly accelerated observer sees thermal radiation at
    T_U = ℏa/(2πck_B). The universe itself has a de Sitter temperature
    T_dS = ℏc√(Λ/3)/(2πk_B). When a particle's acceleration is so low
    that T_U(a) < T_dS, the Unruh effect can no longer distinguish
    the particle's acceleration from the cosmic background. Below this
    threshold, inertia (= resistance to acceleration) is modified.

    Threshold: T_U(a_min) = T_dS → a_min = c√(Λ/3)

    This is the Milgrom-Unruh connection (see also: Verlinde 2016,
    McCulloch 2007, Smolin 2017).

  What it explains:
    - WHY a₀ appears: it is the boundary where local and cosmic
      thermal scales merge
    - Universality: cosmic temperature is the same everywhere
    - Newton limit: T_U >> T_dS means acceleration is "sharp"
      and inertia is standard Newtonian
    - 2π: appears naturally from the Unruh formula

  What it fails:
    - Same base scale problem: a_min = c√(Λ/3) = 0.59 × a₀
    - Mechanism inside galaxies: Unruh radiation is a QFT result for
      uniformly accelerated observers in flat space. Real galaxy
      dynamics involve non-uniform, curved spacetime. The translation
      from "Unruh threshold" to "rotation curve modification" is not
      established.
    - Clusters: no specific prediction
    - If we use H₀ instead of √(Λ/3) in the Unruh argument:
      T_H = ℏH₀/(2πk_B) gives a_min = cH₀ (no 2π in a_min).
      Then a₀ = cH₀, not cH₀/2π. Off by factor 2π in the
      opposite direction. The 2π arithmetic is subtle here.

  Open questions specific to this path:
    - Can the Unruh argument be made rigorous in curved spacetime?
    - Which temperature (T_dS or T_H = ℏH₀/2πk_B) is the right
      cosmic reference? They differ by factor H₀/H_∞ ≈ 1.23.
    - Is modified inertia the right physical picture, or is it

  ──── PATH 3: Dark-Sector Coupling ────

    feedback, self-interaction, phase transition (superfluid DM),
    or dark photon mediation — forces DM halos to track baryon
    distributions below a critical acceleration.

    The scale a₀ emerges from the coupling physics, not from
    cosmology directly. Example: in superfluid DM (Berezhiani &
    Khoury 2015), the superfluid phonon force mimics MOND below
    a critical acceleration set by the DM particle mass and
    self-coupling.

  What it explains:
    - Clusters: DM is real, so clusters work naturally
    - Newton limit: standard gravity + DM at high density
    - Mechanism: has one (coupling/feedback/phase transition)
    - RAR tightness: potentially, if coupling is deterministic

  What it fails:
    - cH₀ coincidence: no natural reason for a₀ ~ cH₀/2π
      The coupling constant would have to be tuned.
    - Universality: coupling depends on formation history.
      Why is the scatter so small?
    - a₀ derivation: in superfluid DM, a₀ depends on DM particle
      parameters (mass, coupling). These are free parameters.
      a₀ is fitted, not derived.
    - Redshift evolution: depends on model specifics. Some predict
      evolution, some don't. Not a clean prediction.

  Open questions specific to this path:
    - Can any DM coupling model predict a₀ ≈ cH₀/2π from particle
      physics parameters alone?
    - Can feedback models reproduce RAR scatter < 0.13 dex?
    - Is superfluid DM phase transition radius the right scale
      to produce the a₀ transition?

PART XXVII: FIRST DERIVATION ATTEMPT — UNRUH / de SITTER, STEP BY STEP

  ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  STEP 1: THE UNRUH EFFECT (established physics)
  ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Result (Unruh 1976, Fulling 1973, Davies 1975):
    A uniformly accelerated observer in Minkowski vacuum sees a
    thermal bath at temperature:

      T_U = ℏa / (2πck_B)

  This is not controversial. It follows from:
    - Quantization of a scalar field in Rindler coordinates
    - The Bogoliubov transformation between Rindler and Minkowski vacua
    - The periodicity of Euclidean time: β = 2πc/a

  Key point: The 2π is NOT inserted by hand.
    It comes from the periodicity condition in imaginary time.
    The Euclidean Rindler metric has period 2πc/a in τ_E.
    The KMS (Kubo-Martin-Schwinger) condition requires β = 1/kT.
    Therefore T = ℏa/(2πck_B).

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ STATUS: The 2π has a rigorous origin in the Unruh effect.         │
  │ It is NOT a patch. It comes from Euclidean periodicity.           │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  STEP 2: THE de SITTER TEMPERATURE (established physics)
  ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Result (Gibbons & Hawking 1977):
    An observer in de Sitter space (Λ > 0) sees a thermal bath at:

      T_dS = ℏH_Λ / (2πk_B)

    where H_Λ = c√(Λ/3) is the asymptotic Hubble parameter.

  This is the same structure as the Unruh effect, but the
  "acceleration" is replaced by c·H_Λ (the surface gravity
  of the cosmological horizon):

      κ_dS = c·H_Λ = c²√(Λ/3)

  Numerically:
    H_Λ = c√(Λ/3) = √(1.11×10⁻⁵² × (3×10⁸)² / 3) = 1.88×10⁻¹⁸ s⁻¹
    T_dS = ℏH_Λ/(2πk_B) = (1.055×10⁻³⁴)(1.88×10⁻¹⁸)/((6.28)(1.381×10⁻²³))
         = 2.28×10⁻³⁰ K

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │ STATUS: de Sitter space has a temperature. This is established.   │
  │ The temperature is ~10⁻³⁰ K. Extremely cold, but not zero.       │
  └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

  STEP 3: THE THRESHOLD ARGUMENT (the key step)
  ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  Conjecture (Milgrom 1999, McCulloch 2007, Verlinde 2016 variants):

    When a particle's acceleration a is large, its Unruh temperature
    T_U(a) >> T_dS. The particle "sees" its own acceleration clearly
    against the cosmic thermal background. Inertia works normally.

    When a drops so low that T_U(a) ≈ T_dS, the Unruh bath merges
    with the cosmic background. The particle can no longer "feel" its
    own acceleration distinctly. Inertia is modified.

    The threshold is:

      T_U(a_min) = T_dS
      ℏa_min/(2πck_B) = ℏH_Λ/(2πk_B)
      a_min = c·H_Λ = c²√(Λ/3)

  Numerically:
    a_min = c²√(Λ/3) = (3×10⁸)² × √(1.11×10⁻⁵² / 3)
          = 9×10¹⁶ × 6.08×10⁻²⁷
          ≈ 5.5×10⁻¹⁰ m/s²

    Wait — let me redo this carefully:
    √(Λ/3) = √(3.7×10⁻⁵³) = 6.08×10⁻²⁷ m⁻¹

    c²√(Λ/3) = 3×10⁸ × 1.82×10⁻¹⁸ = 5.47×10⁻¹⁰ m/s²

    Hmm. That gives 5.5×10⁻¹⁰, not 7.07×10⁻¹¹.

    H_Λ = c√(Λ/3) has units s⁻¹? No.
    √(Λ/3) has units m⁻¹ (since Λ has units m⁻²).
    c√(Λ/3) has units m/s × m⁻¹ = s⁻¹. This is H_Λ.
    The surface gravity κ = cH_Λ = c²√(Λ/3) has units m/s².

    Let me be precise:
    Λ = 1.11×10⁻⁵² m⁻²

    H_Λ = c × 6.08×10⁻²⁷ = 1.825×10⁻¹⁸ s⁻¹

    But c√(Λ/3) has units s⁻¹, not m/s². This was H_Λ, not a_dS.

    CORRECTION: The script in Part XXVI computed c√(Λ/3) with c in m/s

    κ_dS = c²√(Λ/3) = 5.47×10⁻¹⁰ m/s²

  STEP 4: WHERE THE 2π RE-ENTERS
  ════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  In Step 3, the 2π cancelled because both sides had the Unruh form.
  But a₀ ≈ cH₀/2π, which means a factor of 2π IS present in the
  relationship between a₀ and cH₀.

  Where could it come from?

  The threshold gives a_threshold = cH (either H₀ or H_Λ).
  The observation gives a₀ ≈ cH₀/2π.
  The ratio is a₀/a_threshold = 1/2π ≈ 0.16.

  Possible origins of this extra 1/2π:

  Option A: The threshold is not T_U = T_dS, but T_U = T_dS/(2π)
    This would require a physical reason for the extra factor.
    No known argument provides this.

  Option B: The transition is not sharp but gradual.
    If the interpolation function is μ(x) = x/(1+x) where x = a/a₀,
    then the "effective" threshold (where μ = 0.5) is at a = a₀,
    but the onset of deviation begins at a ~ 2π × a₀.
    This reverses the logic: a₀ is the midpoint, not the onset.

  Option C: The relevant cosmic scale is ω = H/(2π), not H itself.
    If H₀ is a frequency f, the angular frequency is ω = 2πf = 2πH₀.
    The Unruh temperature for this angular frequency:
    T = ℏ(cω)/(2πck_B) = ℏω/(2πk_B) = ℏ(2πH₀)/(2πk_B) = ℏH₀/k_B
    The threshold: ℏa/(2πck_B) = ℏH₀/k_B
    a = 2πcH₀ = 4.11×10⁻⁹ m/s². WORSE. 34× a₀.

  Option D: The relationship is not a threshold but a geometric mean.
    a₀ = √(a_N × κ_dS) is the MOND deep limit (Milgrom's formula).
    When a_N = a₀: a₀ = √(a₀ × κ_dS), so a₀ = κ_dS. Circular.
    When a_N → 0: a → √(a_N × a₀) → MOND behavior.
    This doesn't explain a₀ itself, it uses a₀.

  Option E: a₀ appears as cH₀/2π because a₀ = ℏH₀/(2πk_BT₀) × c²
    where T₀ is some reference temperature. But this introduces
    a new free parameter T₀, so it explains nothing.

    a₀ = cH₀/2π ← This matches!

  The Unruh/de Sitter path got closest to explaining the 2π and the
  cosmic connection, but broke at the threshold equation.

  Three options forward:

  1. Fix the threshold argument
     Look for a modified criterion where 2π survives.
     Example: energy comparison instead of temperature comparison.
     E_U = k_B T_U = ℏa/(2πc). E_dS = k_B T_dS = ℏH/(2π).
     Setting E_U/c = ℏa/(2πc²) equal to ℏH/(2πc):
     a/(2πc) = H/(2πc) → a = cH. Same result. 2π still cancels.

  2. Try a different physical picture entirely
     Instead of temperature threshold, try:
     - Holographic entropy bound (bits on the horizon → acceleration)
     - Modified dispersion relation from Λ
     - Vacuum polarization screening at large distances
     These are separate investigations, each needing its own Part.

  3. Accept the factor and focus on the mechanism
     Take a₀ ≈ cH₀/(2π) as empirical (for now), and focus on:
     HOW does any acceleration floor modify galaxy dynamics?
     What is the field equation or force law?
     This skips the derivation problem and attacks the mechanism gap.

  Recommendation: Option 3 is the most productive next step.
  The derivation problem (where does the exact value come from?) may
  require physics we don't yet have. But the mechanism problem (how
  does a₀ affect orbits?) can be attacked with existing tools.

  The question shifts from:
    "Why is a₀ = cH₀/2π?"
  to:
    "Given that a₀ exists, what dynamical equation produces the RAR?"
