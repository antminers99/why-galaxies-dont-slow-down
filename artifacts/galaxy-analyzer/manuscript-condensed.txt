ABSTRACT
================================================================================

We report an empirical acceleration law for galaxy rotation curves that
introduces no extra galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters beyond standard
baryonic mass modeling. Using 175 SPARC galaxies and 22 LITTLE THINGS
dwarfs (4,123 rotation-curve measurements), we find:

(1) A characteristic acceleration scale a_0 ~ 1.2e-10 m/s^2 below which
observed gravity systematically exceeds the baryonic prediction. The
transition is well-described by a single interpolating function of one
variable (g_bar), with 0.198 dex RMS scatter. No tested galaxy property
(mass, size, surface brightness, morphology) reduces the scatter or
modulates a_0.

(2) The observed scale matches the cosmological combination cH_0/2pi to
within ~10%. Given fixed mass-to-light ratios and cosmological inputs,
the resulting equation g_obs = sqrt(g_bar^2 + (cH_0/2pi)^2 * g_bar /
(g_bar + cH_0/2pi)) contains no adjustable per-galaxy parameters and
naturally recovers Newtonian gravity at high acceleration, the Radial
Acceleration Relation (RAR), flat rotation curves, and the Baryonic
Tully-Fisher Relation V^4 = GM*a_0.

(3) A first-pass application to five galaxy clusters shows the same
scale reduces the Newtonian mass deficit by ~43%, but ~52.5% residual
dark matter fraction remains, nearly degenerate with MOND at cluster
scales.

(4) The model makes a unique, falsifiable prediction: if a_0 = cH(z)/2pi,
then the effective acceleration floor should scale with the Hubble
parameter as a_0(z) = a_0 * E(z), implying a_0 is ~70% larger at z=1
and the BTFR normalization shifts upward by ~14%. JWST and ALMA high-z
rotation curves can test this within the next few years.

We present three competing interpretations (modified gravity, baryon-halo
self-regulation, and cosmological acceleration floor) and four
discriminating tests. The equation captures the data; the physics remains
open.

================================================================================
1. INTRODUCTION
================================================================================

Galaxy rotation curves have provided the most direct evidence for the
"missing mass" problem since the work of Rubin & Ford (1970). The
observed rotational velocities in the outer regions of disk galaxies
systematically exceed predictions from the visible baryonic matter,
implying either unseen mass (dark matter) or a modification of
gravitational dynamics.

A remarkable empirical regularity has emerged from large rotation-curve
surveys: the Radial Acceleration Relation (RAR), first quantified by
McGaugh et al. (2016) using the SPARC database. The RAR shows that
observed gravitational acceleration g_obs correlates tightly with baryonic
acceleration g_bar through a single function, with intrinsic scatter of
only ~0.057 dex (Lelli et al. 2017).

The characteristic transition scale a_0 ~ 1.2e-10 m/s^2 has been
recognized since Milgrom (1983) in the context of Modified Newtonian
Dynamics (MOND). A persistent numerical coincidence — that a_0 is close
to cH_0 — has been noted by multiple authors but lacks a consensus
explanation within LCDM.

In this work, we present an empirical formulation that:
  - Captures the RAR with no extra per-galaxy parameters
  - Explicitly ties the transition scale to cH_0/2pi
  - Makes a unique, testable prediction for high-redshift evolution
  - Honestly confronts its limitations at cluster scales

================================================================================
2. DATA AND ASSUMPTIONS
================================================================================

PRIMARY DATASET: SPARC (Lelli et al. 2016)
  175 galaxies with Spitzer 3.6um photometry and multi-survey HI
  3,391 individual rotation-curve points
  Mass-to-light ratios: Y_disk = 0.5 M_sun/L_sun, Y_bulge = 0.7

REPLICATION DATASET: LITTLE THINGS (Oh et al. 2015)
  22 dwarf irregular galaxies
  VLA HI + Spitzer 3.6um
  Independent observers, independent instrument

COMBINED: 197 galaxies, 4,123 data points

BARYONIC MASS MODEL:
  V_bar^2(r) = Y_disk * V_disk^2 + Y_bulge * V_bulge^2 + V_gas^2
  g_bar(r) = V_bar^2(r) / r

COSMOLOGICAL INPUTS:
  c = 2.998e5 km/s
  H_0 = 67.4 km/s/Mpc (Planck 2018)
  G = 4.3009e-6 kpc*(km/s)^2/M_sun

NOTE: The equation is not "parameter-free" in the absolute sense. It
inherits systematic dependence on Y assumptions, distance calibrations,
and gas mass modeling. What it avoids is any per-galaxy free parameter
analogous to a halo concentration or core radius.

================================================================================
3. THE EMPIRICAL ACCELERATION LAW
================================================================================

EQUATION:
  g_obs = sqrt(g_bar^2 + a_0^2 * g_bar / (g_bar + a_0))

where a_0 = cH_0/2pi = 1.08e-10 m/s^2.

LIMITS:
  g_bar >> a_0:  g_obs -> g_bar        (Newtonian regime recovered)
  g_bar << a_0:  g_obs -> sqrt(g_bar*a_0)  (deep MOND / RAR regime)

BTFR DERIVATION:
  In the low-acceleration limit: V^4 = G * M_bar * a_0
  This is the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation, derived rather than assumed.

GLOBAL FIT:
  a_0 = 3702 (km/s)^2/kpc = 1.2e-10 m/s^2
  RMS scatter: 0.198 dex across 175 galaxies

================================================================================
4. UNIVERSALITY TESTS
================================================================================

(a) NO GALAXY-PROPERTY DEPENDENCE:
  Correlation of a_0 with V_max:  r = 0.10
  Correlation of a_0 with R_max:  r = -0.04
  Correlation of a_0 with n_pts:  r = 0.17

  Adding surface brightness dependence Phi(Sigma_bar) makes the fit
  WORSE (cross-validated improvement: -13.35%).

(b) PER-GALAXY vs GLOBAL a_0:
  Per-galaxy fitted a_0 values show large scatter (0.33-0.57 dex by
  mass quartile). This reflects observational/modeling degeneracies,
  not physical variation. The universality claim rests on the global
  RAR fit, not on individual galaxy estimates.

(c) DARK MATTER FRACTION ANTI-CORRELATION:
  f_DM is strongly anti-correlated with Sigma_bar:
    slope b = -0.178, partial r|V_max = -0.656, p < 1e-6
  This persists across all mass bins, inner and outer radii, and
  alternative density definitions.
  Nominal combined excess over LCDM expectations: 5.1 sigma
  (frequentist bootstrap; see caveats in full report).

(d) INDEPENDENT REPLICATION (LITTLE THINGS):
  Same negative slope, same partial correlation direction.
  Robust across Y_disk sensitivity and Monte Carlo tests.

================================================================================
5. RELATION TO cH_0
================================================================================

OBSERVED:   a_0 = 1.2e-10 m/s^2
PREDICTED:  cH_0/2pi = 1.08e-10 m/s^2
MISMATCH:   ~10%

The ratio epsilon = a_0/(cH_0) = 0.176 is close to 1/(2pi) = 0.159.

This numerical coincidence has been noted since Milgrom (1983) but has
no consensus explanation. It could indicate:
  - A deep physical connection between cosmic expansion and local gravity
  - An emergent property of baryon-halo self-regulation in LCDM
  - A numerical coincidence

We adopt the cosmological identification a_0 = cH_0/2pi as a working
hypothesis, which makes the equation fully specified by standard inputs.

================================================================================
6. CLUSTER AND NON-GALACTIC STRESS TESTS
================================================================================

CLUSTER TEST (5 clusters: Coma, Perseus, Bullet, Virgo, Abell 2029):
  The same equation with virial correction (sigma = v_c/sqrt(3)):
  - Newton (baryons only): ~92.3% deficit
  - Floor model: ~52.5% deficit (43% reduction from Newton)
  - MOND: ~52.5% deficit (comparable to Floor)

  The floor brings predictions closer to observed but does not provide
  a distinct or complete cluster-scale solution. It remains nearly
  degenerate with MOND in this regime. Additional physics is required.

BULLET CLUSTER:
  The floor model does not address the spatial offset between dark and
  baryonic mass observed via gravitational lensing, which remains an
  important constraint on any modified gravity interpretation.

================================================================================
7. REDSHIFT PREDICTION (THE KILLER TEST)
================================================================================

If a_0 = cH(z)/2pi, then:
  a_0(z) = a_0 * E(z)
  E(z) = H(z)/H_0 = sqrt(Omega_m*(1+z)^3 + Omega_Lambda)

For Planck cosmology (Omega_m = 0.315):
  At z=1: E = 1.70, so a_0(z=1) = 1.70 * a_0(z=0)

IMPORTANT DISTINCTION:
  - The acceleration floor increases by ~70% at z=1
  - The flat rotation velocity V_flat ~ a_0^(1/4) increases by ~14%
  - These are different quantities and must not be confused

THREE-WAY DISCRIMINATION:
  MOND:          a_0 = constant at all z     (no evolution)
  Cosmic Floor:  a_0 ~ E(z)                 (tracks H(z))
  LCDM/Feedback: no specific prediction      (a_0 is emergent)

  If a_0 rises with z:     CONFIRMS Floor, KILLS MOND
  If a_0 constant at all z: KILLS Floor, CONFIRMS MOND
  If a_0 varies randomly:   KILLS both, supports LCDM

REQUIRED DATA:
  JWST high-z rotation curves (z = 0.5 - 3)
  ALMA gas kinematics at high redshift

================================================================================
8. DISCUSSION: THREE COMPETING INTERPRETATIONS
================================================================================

A. MODIFIED GRAVITY (MOND-like)
  The equation is the fundamental law; gravity changes below a_0.
  + Simple, one law for all galaxies
  + Tight scatter, no formation-history dependence
  - Fails in galaxy clusters
  - No complete relativistic theory
  - Does not explain why a_0 ~ cH_0

B. BARYON-HALO SELF-REGULATION
  Dark matter exists; RAR emerges from CDM + baryonic feedback.
  + Works within standard LCDM
  + Compatible with CMB, large-scale structure
  - Different feedback models give different a_0
  - Simulated RAR scatter exceeds observed
  - No mechanism produces exactly cH_0/2pi

C. COSMIC ACCELERATION FLOOR (This Work)
  a_0 = cH_0/2pi is physical; cosmic expansion creates a minimum
  acceleration floor coupled to local gravity.
  + Explains the cH_0 coincidence
  + Explains universality (set by cosmology, not galaxies)
  + Makes falsifiable high-z prediction
  - No known physical mechanism connects H_0 to galaxy interiors
  - The 1/(2pi) factor lacks rigorous derivation
  - Does not specify whether DM exists or gravity is modified

================================================================================
9. CONCLUSION
================================================================================

Galaxy rotation-curve data from 197 galaxies (4,123 measurements)
strongly support the existence of a characteristic acceleration scale
near 1.2e-10 m/s^2, together with a tight one-variable relation between
baryonic and observed acceleration.

An empirical formula tied to cH_0/2pi reproduces the observed limits and
naturally yields the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation, with no extra
galaxy-by-galaxy tuning parameters given standard baryonic inputs.

However, this remains an empirical law rather than a physical theory, and
its interpretation — modified gravity, baryon-coupled dark sector physics,
or cosmological emergence — remains open. The most discriminating test is
the redshift evolution of a_0: if a_0 tracks H(z), MOND is ruled out;
if a_0 is constant, the cosmic floor hypothesis is ruled out. JWST and
ALMA data within the next few years will settle this question.

A first-pass cluster stress test shows the acceleration floor improves
Newtonian predictions by ~43% but does not eliminate the missing mass,
remaining nearly degenerate with MOND. The Bullet Cluster spatial offset
between dark and baryonic mass is not addressed.

The equation captures the observation. The physics remains open.

Having the right equation is not the same as understanding the physics.
