# Literature References — Galaxy Rotation Curve Analyzer

## Tier 1: Essential Foundation (Must-Know)

1. **Lelli, McGaugh & Schombert (2016a)** — SPARC: Mass Models for 175 Disk Galaxies with Spitzer Photometry and Accurate Rotation Curves. AJ 152, 157. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2016AJ....152..157L)
   - The SPARC database. Our entire dataset.

2. **McGaugh, Lelli & Schombert (2016)** — Radial Acceleration Relation in Rotationally Supported Galaxies. PRL 117, 201101. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2016PhRvL.117t1101M)
   - RAR short paper. gobs = f(gbar) point-by-point. Central to modern debate.

3. **Lelli, McGaugh & Schombert (2017)** — One Law to Rule Them All: The Radial Acceleration Relation of Galaxies. ApJ 836, 152. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2017ApJ...836..152L)
   - RAR full paper. Wider sample, deeper discussion. The key reference.

4. **Lelli, McGaugh & Schombert (2016b)** — The Relation between Stellar and Dynamical Surface Densities in the Central Regions of Disk Galaxies. ApJ 827, L19. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2016ApJ...827L..19L)
   - Central surface density relation. Direct relevance to our Sigma0 analysis.

5. **Oh et al. (2015)** — High-resolution Mass Models of Dwarf Galaxies from LITTLE THINGS. AJ 149, 180. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2015AJ....149..180O)
   - Dwarf galaxy rotation curves. Why dwarfs are a different regime.

## Tier 2: Diversity and Open Problem

6. **Oman et al. (2015)** — The unexpected diversity of dwarf galaxy rotation curves. MNRAS 452, 3650. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2015MNRAS.452.3650O)
   - Diversity problem in dwarfs. Key context for regime boundary.

7. **Santos-Santos et al. (2020)** — Baryonic clues to the puzzling diversity of dwarf galaxy rotation curves. MNRAS 495, 58. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2020MNRAS.495...58S)
   - Baryonic role in diversity. Close to our project line.

8. **Marasco et al. (2020)** — Reconciling the Diversity and Uniformity of Galactic Rotation Curves. (check exact ref). [ADS search needed]
   - Reconciling diversity with uniformity.

## Tier 3: Gas Fraction and Scaling Relations

9. **Catinella et al. (2010)** — The GALEX Arecibo SDSS Survey. I. Gas fraction scaling relations. MNRAS 403, 683. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2010MNRAS.403..683C)
   - MHI/M* vs stellar mass and surface density. Direct background for log(MHI/L36).

10. **Catinella et al. (2018)** — xGASS: total cold gas scaling relations and molecular-to-atomic gas ratios. MNRAS 476, 875. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2018MNRAS.476..875C)
    - Modern cold gas scaling relations. Broader context for our result.

11. **Huang et al. (2012)** — The Arecibo Legacy Fast ALFA Survey: The Galaxy Population Detected by ALFALFA. ApJ 756, 113. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2012ApJ...756..113H)
    - Large HI survey. Gas-stellar connection on large sample.

12. **Cortese et al. (2011)** — The effect of the environment on the HI scaling relations. MNRAS 415, 1797. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2011MNRAS.415.1797C)
    - Environment effects on gas. Future direction for us.

13. **Boselli et al. (2014)** — The effect of structure and star formation on the gas content of nearby galaxies. A&A 564, A67. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2014A%26A...564A..67B)
    - Structure vs star formation vs gas. Directly relevant to our question.

## Tier 4: Tully-Fisher and Baryonic Relations

14. **Verheijen (2001)** — The Ursa Major Cluster of Galaxies. V. HI Rotation Curve Shapes and the Tully-Fisher Relations. ApJ 563, 694. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2001ApJ...563..694V)
    - Classic. Distinguishes Vmax vs Vflat effect on TFR.

15. **Bell & de Jong (2001)** — Stellar Mass-to-Light Ratios and the Tully-Fisher Relation. ApJ 550, 212. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2001ApJ...550..212B)
    - M/L background. Relevant to our Upsilon choices.

16. **Schombert, McGaugh & Lelli (2020)** — Using the Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation to Measure H0. AJ 160, 71. [ADS](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2020AJ....160...71S)
    - Updated BTFR with practical calibrations.

17. **Lelli, McGaugh & Schombert (2019)** — The Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation. I. WISE/Spitzer Calibration. (check exact ref). [ADS search needed]
    - Latest BTFR calibration.

18. **Lelli, McGaugh & Schombert (2019b)** — The Baryonic Tully-Fisher Relation. II. Stellar Mass Models. (check exact ref). [ADS search needed]
    - Stellar mass model effects on BTFR.

## Tier 5: Current Debate and Falsification

    - Challenges RAR universality. Important counterpoint.

20. **Petersen & Lelli (2020)** — A Strong Falsification of the Universal Radial Acceleration Relation. (check exact ref). [ADS search needed]
    - Challenges RAR. Important for context.

21. **Banik et al. (2024)** — On the tension between the radial acceleration relation and Solar system quadrupole in modified gravity MOND. (check exact ref). [ADS search needed]
    - MOND tension. Shows debate is not closed.

22. **Renzo's rule revisited** — A statistical study of galaxies' baryon-dynamics coupling. (recent, check exact ref). [ADS search needed]
    - Baryon features in rotation curves. Close to our project line.
