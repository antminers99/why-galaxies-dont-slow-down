## What remains unchanged

- **H exists.** Bilateral VfResid–a0Resid coupling: r ≈ 0.77 (LOO, p < 0.001, N = 55), replicated on N = 59 independent galaxies.
- **Not a distance artifact.** Construction-independent (55/55 recipe variants), distance-independent, externally replicated.
- **1D information ceiling is real.** 88.5% of H is inaccessible from rotation-curve features alone, regardless of representation.
- **Red team: 11/11 pass** (8/8 critical), including independent pipeline replication (r = 0.91).

## What changed

- **m = 2 is no longer the unique carrier.** Program 11 (16-bin azimuthal decomposition) shows m = 3 carries 58% of non-rotational power vs. m = 2 at 11%. The original r(DQ, m2) = 0.847 was partly inflated by azimuthal aliasing (8 bins, Nyquist ≈ m = 3).

## New formulation

- **Old (v14):** "H is carried by the outer-halo m = 2 velocity-field mode → halo triaxiality."
- **New (v15):** "H is carried by angular velocity-field complexity — the total non-axisymmetric structure in 2D kinematics, not a single Fourier mode."

## Why the change

- Program 11 Test A: Radial m = 2 gradient is universal across all 7 THINGS galaxies (r = −0.26, p = 0.49) — not DQ-dependent.
- Program 11 Test B: m = 3 and m = 5 correlate with DQ at r = 0.786 and r = 0.802, exceeding m = 2 at r = 0.516.
- Cause: 8-bin azimuthal decomposition has Nyquist limit near m = 3, causing higher-mode power to alias into the m = 2 measurement.

---

**Bottom line:** The detection of H is robust. Only the physical interpretation of the 2D carrier broadened — from one specific mode to the full non-axisymmetric complexity of the velocity field. CDM remains consistent but is not uniquely favored.
